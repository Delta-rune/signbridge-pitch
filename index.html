Synora Presents

SIGNVOICE AI

Removing communication barriers in public spaces with real-time AI

THE VISION

SIGNVOICE EMPOWERS THE DEAF AND MUTE COMMUNITY TO COMMUNICATE EFFORTLESSLY IN HOSPITALS, BANKS, AND LOCAL SHOPS—RECLAIMING THEIR INDEPENDENCE THROUGH SEAMLESS, REAL-TIME AI.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Capture & tracking

Computer Vision (OpenCV) captures the live feed. MediaPipe extracts hand landmark coordinates in real-time.

2. Gesture classification

A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in TensorFlow analyzes the landmarks to identify and classify the sign.

3. Sign → Audio

The classified sign is instantly converted into spoken audio using native Text-to-Speech (TTS) libraries.

4. Audio → Sign

The shopkeeper’s spoken reply is converted to text (STT) and displayed as clear sign language animations.

ADVANTAGES

  • Instant independence in shops, banks, and schools.
  • Reduced social isolation and increased inclusion.
  • Low-cost, mobile-deployed digital interpreter.
  • Learning Mode assists others in learning sign language.

GLOBAL SCALABILITY

Cloud Synchronization

User accounts sync custom settings and saved quick phrases across multiple devices seamlessly.

Global Translation

Scalable architecture designed to translate regional signs into major international spoken languages.

CHALLENGES

Environmental dependency

Computer vision accuracy depends on lighting conditions and camera quality in public hubs.

Dialect variance

Sign language (ISL) varies regionally, requiring specialized datasets for localized accuracy.
